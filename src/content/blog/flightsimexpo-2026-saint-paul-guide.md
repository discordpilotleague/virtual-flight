FlightSimExpo is the largest enthusiast flight simulation convention in the world, and the 2026 edition is heading to Saint Paul, Minnesota from June 12 to 14. After successful stops in Las Vegas, Orlando, Houston, San Diego and Providence, the show now expects close to 3,000 attendees, 80+ exhibitors and dozens of seminars across three packed days. This complete guide gives you everything you need to plan your trip: dates, venue, tickets, hotels, transport, schedule and the exhibitors confirmed so far.

## What is FlightSimExpo?

FlightSimExpo is the annual gathering of the global flight simulation community, organized by the [Flight Simulation Association](https://flightsimassociation.com/events). Launched in 2018, the event has welcomed more than 10,000 attendees over its first eight editions and has grown into the must-attend show for anyone passionate about Microsoft Flight Simulator, X-Plane, Prepar3D, DCS World, home cockpits or VR aviation.

The format mixes three things you rarely find in one place. First, an exhibit hall where developers, hardware vendors and online networks like VATSIM and IVAO show their latest products and let you try them hands-on. Second, an extensive program of seminars, workshops and Q&A sessions where studios announce new releases and explain their roadmaps. Third, a community side: 5K fun runs, banquet dinners, brewery tours and aviation field trips that turn the weekend into something closer to a festival than a trade show.

## When and where: Saint Paul RiverCentre, June 12-14, 2026

The 2026 edition takes place at the **Saint Paul RiverCentre**, located at 150 W Kellogg Blvd, Saint Paul, MN 55102. The venue sits in downtown Saint Paul, at the intersection of Interstate 94 and Interstate 35, and is only a 15-minute drive from Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport (MSP). MSP is itself a four-hour or shorter flight from any major city in the contiguous United States, which makes Saint Paul one of the most accessible host cities the show has ever picked.

The show runs across three days with very different rhythms:

- **Friday, June 12** (12:00 PM–7:00 PM) — known as **#FSExpoFriday**: this is the announcement day. From noon to 4:30 PM, major developers reveal new aircraft, sceneries and tools. From 4:30 PM to 7:00 PM, the exhibit hall opens for an evening preview.
- **Saturday, June 13** (9:00 AM–6:00 PM) — full exhibit hall day, with seminars, workshops, demos and panels running across multiple stages.
- **Sunday, June 14** (9:00 AM–3:00 PM) — second full day of the show before the wrap-up.

Thursday June 11 is reserved for exhibitor setup and is not open to the public.

## How to get there and where to stay

MSP airport is the obvious arrival point. From the airport you can either rent a car, take a rideshare to downtown Saint Paul (about 15 minutes off-peak), or use the Metro Transit Blue and Green Lines which connect MSP to downtown Saint Paul. Driving in from Chicago, Milwaukee or other Midwest cities is also realistic — Saint Paul is well served by the I-94 / I-35 corridor.

Parking at the RiverCentre is paid: expect **$20 to $35 per day** depending on event volume, with payment by QR code at the ramps or nearby pay stations. Several other downtown garages are available within a short walk if the venue lots fill up.

For accommodation, FlightSimExpo has negotiated rates with four official partner hotels, all within walking distance of the venue:

- **Hampton Inn and Suites by Hilton St. Paul Downtown** — $155 per night, 2-minute walk, complimentary breakfast, valet parking $36/night. Booking deadline May 28.
- **Courtyard by Marriott St. Paul Downtown** — $164 per night, 3-minute walk, valet parking $20/night. Booking deadline May 11 (currently sold out, check for cancellations).
- **InterContinental Saint Paul Riverfront** — $159 per night, 8-minute walk, $199 with valet parking included. Booking deadline May 19.
- **Hyatt Place St. Paul Downtown** — $159 per night, 15-minute walk, complimentary breakfast, valet parking $24/night. Booking deadline May 28.

All four impose a minimum check-in age of 18 for the room block. The full booking links are on the official [hotels page](https://flightsimexpo.com/hotels). Book early — the 2-minute Hampton Inn and the InterContinental traditionally fill up first.

## What to expect on the show floor: 60+ exhibitors and the developers behind your sim

FlightSimExpo 2026 already has a confirmed lineup of more than 60 exhibitors, with three sponsorship tiers above the rest of the floor. The **Diamond+ tier** features [Navigraph](https://navigraph.com/), [PMDG Simulations](https://pmdg.com/) and [iniBuilds](https://www.inibuilds.com/) — three of the most influential names in modern flight simulation, responsible for some of the most-flown aircraft in MSFS 2024 and X-Plane 12.

The **Diamond tier** includes OnAir Airline Manager, SayIntentions.AI, FSElite, SKALARKI Electronics, WINCTRL, Meridian GMT, SimFab, FSReborn, Thrustmaster, Honeycomb Aeronautical and X-Plane itself. The **Platinum tier** brings DOF Reality Motion Simulators, VA Systems, AviaSim, SIMiONIC, Gleim Aviation and Parallel 42. And the **Gold tier** is where the long tail of community-favorite brands sits: Aerosoft, FlyByWire Simulations, MK Studios, BlueBird Simulations, Tobii Gaming, Sporty's Pilot Shop, TFDi Design, SoFly, Pimax, GeoFS, VATSIM and many more.

Beyond software and hardware, the floor also hosts real-world aviation organizations, virtual airlines, training programs and community groups. It's the only place each year where you can compare a six-degrees-of-freedom motion platform against a sub-$300 yoke set, plug a Honeycomb Bravo into a friend's home cockpit, and then walk three minutes to talk roadmap with a PMDG developer. That density of expertise is the whole point of going.

## Seminars, workshops and the legendary Friday reveals

Two stages run continuously on Saturday and Sunday with seminars and panels, while the third stage is reserved for hands-on workshops. Past editions have covered every angle of the hobby: how to configure home cockpits, getting started on VATSIM, scenery development with the MSFS SDK, helicopter procedures in DCS, building a virtual airline, real-world ATC career paths, and deep technical dives from developer teams.

Friday afternoon is something else entirely. Major studios use the **#FSExpoFriday** announcement block to reveal new products, often with worldwide first looks at upcoming aircraft, scenery packages or platform updates. If you only have one day to attend, Friday is usually the most newsworthy. The 2025 edition in Providence featured 40+ seminars and announcements over the weekend — 2026 is expected to match or exceed that.

Add-on experiences are also bookable on top of your pass: the **MSP Airside Tour**, the **Delta TechOps Tour**, simulator training classes, a 5K fun run, a banquet dinner, a brewery tour and even canoeing. These extras sell out fast, so book them at registration if you're interested.

## Who should attend FlightSimExpo?

If you fly Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024, X-Plane 12, Prepar3D, DCS World or any combat or civilian sim platform, you'll find your tribe. The show genuinely caters to four overlapping audiences: hobbyist simmers who want to try gear before buying, hardcore home-cockpit builders looking for parts and integration tips, real-world pilots and student pilots using sims for training and procedure rehearsal, and developers (hardware and software) who use the weekend to gather feedback and recruit beta testers.

You don't need to be an expert. A surprising portion of attendees are first-timers who fell in love with flight sim during the pandemic and are coming to see, in person, the people whose products they've been using for years. The energy on the floor — first-time visitors, aviation pros, YouTubers, developers, virtual airline crews — is what makes the weekend memorable.

## Practical checklist before you go

- Buy your **pass before May 1** to lock in early-bird pricing.
- Book your hotel **before mid-May** (Hampton Inn and Courtyard go first).
- Plan your Friday around the **#FSExpoFriday announcements** — this is where the news happens.
- Register for **add-on tours and workshops** at the same time as your pass.
- Bring a **notebook and a phone with plenty of storage**: you'll be photographing and noting down a lot.
- If you're coming from outside North America, check that your **MSP arrival** lands by Thursday evening so you're rested for Friday's noon kickoff.

## Final thoughts

FlightSimExpo 2026 in Saint Paul looks set to be the biggest, most logistically friendly edition the show has ever run. The convention center is downtown, the airport is fifteen minutes away, the official hotels are all walkable, and the exhibitor list is as strong as any past edition. Whether you go for the announcements, the hardware, the seminars or just to meet the community in person, three days at the RiverCentre is one of the best returns on time and money the flight sim hobby offers all year.
