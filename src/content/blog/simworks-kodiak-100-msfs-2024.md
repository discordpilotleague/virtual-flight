Few add-on aircraft have earned the kind of loyalty that the [SimWorks Studios](https://simworksstudios.com/) Kodiak 100 commands. For years it has been one of the most recommended turboprop add-ons in the MSFS ecosystem -- a genuinely versatile bush plane that performs as convincingly on a gravel strip in Alaska as it does flying a scheduled island-hopper in the Maldives. On March 27, 2026, SimWorks Studios released the Kodiak 100 for Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024, as [reported by FSElite](https://fselite.net/content/simworks-kodiak-100-for-msfs-2024-released-now/), and this is not a port. It is a ground-up rework that takes advantage of every major platform feature MSFS 2024 offers, from the native walk-around system to the Career Mode integration. The amphibian variant, previously sold separately, is now included in the base package.

## A Complete Systems Overhaul

The headline improvement is depth. SimWorks Studios has rewritten the Kodiak's core systems from scratch, and the result is an aircraft that behaves less like a game object and more like a machine with thousands of interconnected components doing their jobs.

### Electrical System

The electrical system has been completely rewritten with working circuit breakers that actually affect downstream systems. Pull a breaker and the associated equipment goes offline. This is not cosmetic -- it creates meaningful failure scenarios and rewards pilots who understand their aircraft's electrical architecture. For simmers who enjoy working through emergency checklists, this level of fidelity makes a real difference.

### Environmental Control System

The Environmental Control System has been reworked with a redrawn air conditioning interface. Cabin temperature management now involves real logic rather than abstract sliders, and the system interacts with bleed air and electrical loads in a way that reflects the real Kodiak's design. It is a subtle feature, but it contributes to the sense that every switch in the cockpit does something meaningful.

### Engine and Turbine Modeling

The Pratt & Whitney PT6A-34 turboprop receives careful attention. SimWorks Studios has implemented a Smart AutoStart system that handles the turbine start sequence with built-in hot-start avoidance. Pilots who prefer manual starts can still do everything by hand, but the automated option eliminates the risk of cooking the engine during a rushed startup -- a common mistake with turboprop aircraft in the simulator. Temperature monitoring, fuel flow, and torque indications all respond with the kind of inertia and lag that turbine operators expect.

## Avionics: G1000 NXi Done Right

The Kodiak 100 ships with a full Garmin G1000 NXi suite, and SimWorks Studios has gone well beyond the default implementation. Custom screens have been developed specifically for the Kodiak, including an accurate Weight & Balance page that calculates center of gravity based on actual fuel load, passenger count, and cargo placement. This is not a novelty feature -- it is a practical tool that affects how you load the aircraft and whether your takeoff performance numbers are reliable.

The ESI500 standby instrument is modeled with synthetic vision capability, providing a backup attitude and heading reference that mirrors the real unit's functionality. In IMC conditions or during a primary display failure, having a synthetic vision standby display is not just immersive -- it is operationally useful.

SimWorks Studios has confirmed that Navigraph chart integration within the G1000 is planned for a future update, which will allow pilots to view approach plates and SID/STAR charts directly on the MFD without switching to an external application. Combined with the already-functional flight planning and navigation features, the G1000 NXi in this Kodiak represents one of the most complete implementations of the avionics suite available in MSFS 2024.

## Walk-Around and Exterior Detail

MSFS 2024 introduced a native walk-around inspection system, and SimWorks Studios has embraced it fully. Pilots can now perform a genuine pre-flight inspection by walking around the aircraft and interacting with physical elements: open and remove engine covers, inspect brake assemblies and wheel condition, check oil levels, and examine the propeller. This transforms the pre-flight from a perfunctory checklist into an engaging procedure that builds situational awareness before you even start the engine.

The exterior model itself receives significant upgrades. The engine bay is fully modeled, meaning you can open the cowling and see the PT6 in its cradle rather than staring at a flat texture. The cargo pod interior is likewise detailed -- open it up and you will find modeled cargo space rather than a void. The propeller has been redrawn with new graphics that capture blade twist and surface finish more convincingly than the MSFS 2020 version.

A native dirt and weathering system adds visual storytelling to the aircraft. Fly the Kodiak hard into unpaved strips and it will show. Mud accumulates on the gear and lower fuselage, and wear patterns appear over time. It is a small detail that contributes enormously to the sense that this is your aircraft, with your operational history written into its paint.

Inside the cabin, animated passengers add life to the experience. They are not static mannequins -- they react and move in ways that remind you this aircraft exists to carry people to places that other aircraft cannot reach.

## The Amphibian Variant

One of the most welcome decisions SimWorks Studios has made with this release is bundling the amphibian variant into the base package. In the MSFS 2020 era, the float-equipped Kodiak was sold as a separate product. Now it comes standard, and it transforms the aircraft's operational envelope. The amphibian Kodiak opens up water-based operations across the simulator's vast coastlines, rivers, and lakes. With MSFS 2024's improved water physics and surface rendering, landing on a remote alpine lake or a sheltered bay feels more convincing than ever.

The float installation is not merely cosmetic. It affects weight, drag, and handling characteristics in the way you would expect -- the amphibian is heavier on its feet, slower to accelerate, and requires different approach technique compared to the wheeled variant. Transitioning between water and land operations involves managing the retractable gear on the floats, adding a layer of procedural awareness that keeps the flying interesting.

## Career Mode and Marketplace

For pilots invested in MSFS 2024's Career Mode, the Kodiak 100 is fully compatible when purchased through the in-sim Marketplace. This means the aircraft can be used in the Career progression system, earning income from bush flights, cargo runs, and passenger charters. The Kodiak's versatility makes it an ideal Career Mode workhorse -- it can land practically anywhere, carry a meaningful payload, and operate from strips that would reject larger turboprops.

SimWorks Studios has also confirmed that SU5 compatibility is on the roadmap, ensuring the Kodiak will remain current as the platform evolves.

## Pricing and Availability

The Kodiak 100 for MSFS 2024 is priced at EUR 34.99 (approximately USD 46) and is available from the SimWorks Studios store and Contrail. Owners of the MSFS 2020 Kodiak receive a 50% loyalty discount, bringing the upgrade cost to approximately EUR 17.50. Given that the amphibian variant is now included -- a product that was previously a separate purchase -- the effective value proposition is strong even at full price.

## Verdict

The SimWorks Studios Kodiak 100 was already one of the most respected turboprop add-ons in the MSFS ecosystem. This MSFS 2024 release elevates it further with rewritten systems, native platform integration, and the bundled amphibian variant. The electrical system overhaul, the detailed walk-around, the enhanced G1000 NXi, and the dirt weathering system all contribute to an aircraft that rewards attentive flying and repeated use.

Bush flying remains one of the most popular activities in flight simulation, and the Kodiak 100 is purpose-built for exactly that mission. It is rugged enough for backcountry strips, capable enough for light commercial operations, and deep enough in its systems modeling to keep experienced simmers engaged over hundreds of hours. With Career Mode compatibility and a clear development roadmap that includes Navigraph integration and expanded pre-flight features, the Kodiak 100 is positioned to be a long-term staple of the MSFS 2024 hangar.

Whether you are a returning Kodiak owner taking advantage of the loyalty discount or a new pilot looking for a versatile first turboprop, this is an easy recommendation.
