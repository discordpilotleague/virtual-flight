The A340-600, the longest of Airbus's quad-engine family, had until now been the glaring absence from the MSFS catalogue. Aerosoft finally fills that gap with the **Aircraft A340-600 Pro**, a payware add-on developed in partnership with **ToLiss** — the team already renowned for its benchmark Airbus aircraft on X-Plane. Priced at roughly €80 and available now on the [Aerosoft shop](https://www.aerosoft.com/en/shop/flight/microsoft-flight-simulator/msfs-2020/msfs-aircraft/4763/aerosoft-aircraft-a340-600-pro), this module aims to join the elite of long-haul simulation alongside the PMDG 777 and the Fenix A320. Compatible with **MSFS 2020** and **MSFS 2024**, it targets demanding virtual pilots who want a study-level quadjet for the world's longest routes.

![Aerosoft A340-600 Pro in Lufthansa livery on taxiway — MSFS](/images/aerosoft-a340-600-pro/hero.webp)

## A 3D model worthy of the airframe

At 75.36 metres of fuselage, the A340-600 is a massive aircraft — and Aerosoft has clearly risen to the challenge. The exterior model, built from technical documentation, delivers a level of detail that holds up under close inspection: rivets, antennas, gear doors, static ports — everything is accounted for. Control surface animations — slats, flaps, spoilers, airbrakes — deploy progressively and realistically, far removed from the binary animations found on default aircraft.

Wing flex deserves a special mention. It reacts dynamically to aircraft weight, fuel load and turbulence — a visual detail that transforms the external view in cruise, with the wings gently oscillating just like the real aircraft. The RAM Air Turbine (RAT), passenger and cargo doors, a lockable cockpit door and opening cockpit windows complete the picture.

![Overhead view of the A340-600 Lufthansa at the gate — MSFS](/images/aerosoft-a340-600-pro/cockpit.webp)

## A study-level cockpit by ToLiss

The cockpit is where the ToLiss collaboration truly pays off. The flight deck is fully modelled in high-resolution 3D, with dynamic lighting and interactive animations on every button, rotary and switch. The centre pedestal, with its four thrust levers, is particularly refined — a constant reminder that you are flying a quadjet, a species on the verge of extinction in commercial aviation.

![A340-600 Pro cockpit on night approach — MSFS](/images/aerosoft-a340-600-pro/exterior.webp)

### FMGS and navigation

The Flight Management and Guidance System (FMGS) relies on three independent MCDUs and two independent autopilots. Vertical navigation includes step climbs, and the FMS supports custom waypoints, navaids and runways. A424 leg support places this implementation on par with the best add-ons on the market.

### 303 injectable failures

The number speaks for itself: **303 system failures** are modelled and can be triggered manually or randomly. From hydraulic failures to engine shutdowns, from electrical bus faults to pressurisation anomalies, every system can fail independently. Reset switches are functional, allowing crews to work through failures using real-world procedures — an exceptional training tool. Ten scenarios are included, ranging from straightforward approaches to complex emergencies requiring correct pilot intervention.

![A340-600 Pro cockpit, pilot seats and overhead panel view — MSFS](/images/aerosoft-a340-600-pro/cabin.webp)

## Deep system modelling

The list of simulated systems is staggering and clearly sets the A340-600 Pro apart from mid-range add-ons:

- **Flight control laws** — Four modes simulated: Normal, Alternate 1, Alternate 2 and Direct, with realistic degradation on failure
- **Engines** — Custom model reproducing N1, N2, N3, EGT and fuel flow individually for each of the four Rolls-Royce Trent 556 engines
- **Hydraulics** — Three circuits with flow rates and pressure-loss modelling
- **Electrical** — Buses with automatic reconfiguration on failure
- **Pneumatic** — Bleed air system with pressure-loss calculations
- **Fuel** — Eight tanks with cross-feed capability
- **ADIRU** — Custom model with simulated measurement-accuracy errors
- **Flight control actuators** — Electro-hydraulic servo simulation
- **Braking** — Brake temperature modelling
- **Radio navigation** — Custom model accounting for antenna positioning

For communications, the A340-600 Pro integrates **CPDLC via Hoppie** with PDC and weather data support, as well as **ACARS with SimBrief integration** — two features that have become essential for pilots flying on VATSIM and IVAO.

![A340-600 centre pedestal with four thrust levers — MSFS](/images/aerosoft-a340-600-pro/night.webp)

## EFB and onboard tools

The integrated Electronic Flight Bag (EFB) goes well beyond simple chart display. It offers **Navigraph** and **SimBrief** integration, a weight-and-balance calculator with centre-of-gravity predictions, takeoff and landing performance calculators, and interactive checklists. Situation save and load is supported, with an autosave mode.

For ease of use, Aerosoft provides four preset aircraft states (cold & dark, engines running, etc.), quick engine and APU start, the ability to freeze systems while paused, a choice between imperial and metric units, and a waypoint-skip function for the longest cruise legs. Both the Standard and High Gross Weight variants are included.

## Passenger cabin and visual immersion

Aerosoft chose to fully model the passenger cabin — a rare addition for a study-level add-on, where development effort typically focuses on the cockpit. Seats, individual IFE screens, overhead bins and the centre aisle are all rendered with care. Combined with the lockable cockpit door and opening windows, the result is complete visual immersion from nose to tail.

![A340-600 Pro passenger cabin with IFE screens — MSFS](/images/aerosoft-a340-600-pro/efb.webp)

## 19 liveries and a taxi camera

The package includes **19 liveries** covering a broad range of historic and current A340-600 operators:

- **Lufthansa** (new and legacy livery) — the benchmark -600 operator
- **Qatar Airways**, **Etihad**, **Cathay Pacific** — long-haul carriers from the Gulf and Asia
- **Iberia** (legacy livery), **Virgin Atlantic** (classic) — Europe
- **South African Airways**, **Thai Airways**, **Hainan**, **China Eastern** — worldwide coverage
- **Mahan Air**, **Conviasa**, **Azman Air**, **Plus Ultra**, **Sky Prime**, **Universal Sky Carrier** — niche operators
- **Azerbaijan Government** — VIP configuration
- **Aerosoft House D-AERO** — fictional house livery

A taxi camera using the MSFS Map API rounds out the feature set — a practical tool for manoeuvring a 75-metre aircraft in tight airport spaces.

## System requirements and pricing

The A340-600 Pro demands a solid rig: **Intel i7-10700K** or **Ryzen 7 5700** minimum, **RTX 2080** or **RX 5700**, **32 GB of RAM**, **8 GB of VRAM** and **18 GB of disk space**. High requirements, but consistent with the depth of the simulation. Installation runs through **Aerosoft One** and requires an Aerosoft account.

The retail price is **€80.66** including tax (currently on sale at roughly €70.58). In the context of the study-level airliner market for MSFS — the PMDG 777 and the Fenix A320 sit in the same bracket — the price is competitive, especially given the volume of included content (19 liveries, 303 failures, 10 scenarios, passenger cabin).

## What this means for MSFS

The arrival of the A340-600 Pro in the MSFS ecosystem fills a void that virtual pilots have been waiting for. The long-haul quadjet — a category of aircraft increasingly rare in real skies — now has a study-level simulation that makes no compromises on system depth. The Aerosoft-ToLiss collaboration brings ToLiss's proven expertise in Airbus system modelling together with Aerosoft's strengths in 3D modelling and distribution.

For pilots who dream of flying Singapore to New York or Johannesburg to Atlanta at the controls of a quadjet, the A340-600 Pro finally makes that possible in MSFS with a level of fidelity that justifies the investment.
