Asobo and Microsoft published the April 2026 Development Update for Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024 on April 15, consolidating news from the developer livestream into one comprehensive post. This edition is particularly dense: Sim Update 5 gets a release window, the PlayStation 5 Marketplace and PSVR2 support finally ship, and the twelve-month aircraft roadmap is now clearly laid out — with two Expert Series aircraft that will define the second half of 2026.

## Sim Update 5 lands April 20-24, PSVR2 support goes live

The headline date is a release window: **April 20-24, 2026** for Sim Update 5 across PC, Xbox Series X|S, PlayStation 5 and PS5 Pro. The team is working to resolve two or three remaining "must-fix" bugs before shipping. The rollout is synchronized across all platforms, which avoids the Xbox/PC desync that frustrated players during previous updates.

The big functional addition is a fully-featured **weather radar developed by Working Title**, including tilt, a long-standing community request. For operators of tube-liners and business jets with working radar displays, this closes a major realism gap.

The other major headline is the **PlayStation 5 Marketplace opening with PSVR2 support**. Rather than landing as a single monolithic catalog, third-party add-ons must pass Sony's platform and performance requirements before being listed. An initial batch is already approved, and more will follow over the coming months.

## Aircraft & Avionics Update 4: five aircraft in April

AAU4 ships alongside SU5 and is packed. The lineup mixes serious complex aircraft and heritage flying:

- **ATR 42/72-600** — Expert Series 01, the flagship of the update
- **Antonov An-2** — Famous Flyer 5, the legendary biplane workhorse
- **Fokker F.VII** — Local Legend 2
- **Savoia-Marchetti S.55 / S.55X** — Local Legend 4, the iconic Italian flying boat
- **Latécoère 631** — Local Legend 9

![ATR 42/72-600 Expert Series 01 for MSFS 2024](/images/msfs-devupdate-april-2026/atr.webp)
*Credit: [Microsoft Flight Simulator](https://www.flightsimulator.com/development-update-april-2026/)*

The **ATR 42/72-600** is the one most virtual pilots will want. As the first aircraft in the Expert Series line, it sets the bar for what Asobo considers study-level simulation in-house. Expect full FMS implementation, accurate systems modeling, and the quirky PW127 turboprop handling that makes the real aircraft a challenge to fly smoothly.

## World Update 21 Australia arrives in May

Slated for May 2026, **World Update 21: Australia** is one of the most ambitious WUs of the year:

- **30+ new Areas of Interest** including photogrammetry of major cities
- **6 new bespoke airports** hand-crafted by the dev team
- **29 new Points of Interest**
- A full activity pack: 3 Discovery Flights, 4 Precision Landings, 3 Landing Challenges, 1 Bush Trip, 2 Low Altitude Challenges, 2 Rally Race Challenges

Australia has been a community favorite for years thanks to its bush-flying playground across the Outback and the scenic density of the east coast. Alongside the update, **Local Legend 24: CAC Boomerang** — the WWII-era Australian fighter built by **iniBuilds** — lands in the same month, tying the content release to the regional theme.

## Expert Series 02 Piper M600 in June, Expert Series 03 Praetor 600 in November

This is where the roadmap gets interesting for serious simmers. Two major aircraft anchor the rest of 2026.

**Expert Series 02 — Piper M600** (June 2026) is developed by **FSReborn** and features the **Garmin G3000** suite in collaboration with Working Title. It ships with simulated system failures, the HALO (emergency autoland) system, three interior configurations, and adjustable difficulty/realism sliders. That difficulty slider is notable: Asobo is explicitly addressing the gap between newcomers and expert simmers without forking the product.

**Expert Series 03 — Embraer Praetor 600** (November 2026) is co-developed by **Asobo and Working Title**, and the specification list sets new expectations for in-sim business jets:

- Fully simulated **electrical architecture with circuit breakers**
- Advanced **hydraulics with physics-based behavior**
- Engineering-grade turbine simulation
- **Dual independent FMS units**
- IRS drift modeling

![Embraer Praetor 600 Expert Series 03 for MSFS 2024](/images/msfs-devupdate-april-2026/praetor.webp)
*Credit: [Microsoft Flight Simulator](https://www.flightsimulator.com/development-update-april-2026/)*

IRS drift modeling in particular is a level of detail normally reserved for third-party study-level aircraft. If Asobo and Working Title deliver on all of this, the Praetor will set a new in-house benchmark.

## Community Fly-in Friday and FlightSimExpo 2026

Beyond aircraft and scenery, the update confirms two community beats. **Community Fly-in Friday** continues every Friday at **11:00 AM Pacific / 18:00 UTC**, open to all skill levels and flying new locations every week — a good entry point for new pilots looking to meet the community without the intimidation of VATSIM.

**FlightSimExpo 2026** takes place **June 12-14** in Minneapolis-Saint Paul. The team has confirmed that the **Expert Series 02 Piper M600 announcement** will be made there, which strongly suggests a surprise or two on top — Asobo traditionally saves at least one announcement for the show floor.

## What it means for virtual pilots

This Development Update is the clearest roadmap we've had since the launch of MSFS 2024. The combination of PSVR2 support, the ATR 42/72-600 as a first-party Expert aircraft, the Praetor 600 with study-level systems, and a confirmed Australia update gives serious simmers a full year of high-quality content to look forward to. The new "Development Update after each Developer Stream + Weekly Briefings" cadence also tightens the communication loop — which, after three years of uneven dev communication on MSFS 2020, is a genuinely meaningful shift.

For the full details, the [original April 2026 Development Update on flightsimulator.com](https://www.flightsimulator.com/development-update-april-2026/) is worth reading end-to-end, especially the community Q&A section where Jörg Neumann addresses long-running questions around performance, VR, and the Marketplace catalog.
