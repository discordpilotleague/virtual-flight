## What you get in the box

The MSFS Edition is the same hardware as the regular T.Flight HOTAS One, with an **official Microsoft Flight Simulator livery** — branded colors, MSFS logo on the throttle, packaging tuned to the sim — plus a **one-month Xbox Game Pass** subscription included with the unit. Inside the box you will find the HOTAS itself, a hex key (for swapping the rudder lock), the warranty and instruction documentation. Box weight is around 2 kg; the assembled footprint sits at roughly **350 × 200 × 235 mm**.

The hardware is built around a **complete HOTAS layout**: hand-on-throttle, hand-on-stick. The throttle is **fully detachable** — useful if you want to mount it on your other hand or anchor it to a chair side. A weighted base keeps the unit planted on a desk, and the entire rig is light enough to fly with on your lap on the sofa, which is more relevant for Xbox players than the marketing usually admits.

## The control surface, in detail

The control set was designed for entry-level sim flying but covers more than the price tag implies:

- **5 axes** — pitch, roll, throttle, plus a **dual rudder system** (Z-axis twist on the stick, or progressive tilting lever at the base; you pick which one)
- **14 buttons** spread across the stick and the throttle
- **1 rapid trigger** under the index finger
- **1 multi-directional hat switch** for view-look around or trim
- **10-bit precision** on the main axes — enough resolution that fine elevator inputs at cruise don't feel staircased
- **Adjustable resistance** on the joystick — you can tighten or loosen the center spring to match airliners (lighter) or fighters (firmer)

The dual rudder system is the design choice that defines this product. **Twist the stick** if you do not own pedals; **tilt the progressive lever at the base** if you find twist motion contaminating your roll inputs. Power users invariably end up adding a separate set of pedals (the T.Flight HOTAS One is explicitly compatible with **Thrustmaster TFRP** pedals, sold separately), but the integrated rudder is a real upgrade over keyboard-only steering.

The throttle is potentiometer-based with a smooth, even pull. There is no detent gate for reverse and no afterburner click, which makes sense for an MSFS-branded product targeted at general aviation and airliners rather than fast jets.

## What it changes for virtual pilots

For pilots stepping up from mouse-and-keyboard, the HOTAS One is the difference between **flying with two thumbs** and **flying with two hands**. The dedicated throttle alone reshapes how you fly approaches — instead of tapping F2/F3 you slide thrust precisely; instead of toggling spoilers off a binding you assign them to a stick button you can reach without looking. Within a couple of hours you stop thinking about controls and start flying the aircraft.

## Where the MSFS Edition belongs in 2026

The flight sim hardware market has moved on since the original HOTAS One launched, with high-end Honeycomb Alpha/Bravo combos, Virpil and VKB sticks at the premium tier. The HOTAS One does not pretend to compete with those. **What it still owns is the sub-$120 bracket** — and that bracket is where most new virtual pilots actually shop.

Compared to mid-range alternatives like the Logitech X52, the Thrustmaster has fewer buttons but a noticeably better stick feel and a cleaner throttle action. Against pure-stick options like the Logitech 3D Pro, the HOTAS One wins on the throttle alone. Our wider [joystick and yoke hardware review](/blog/hardware-review-joystick-yoke) puts these options side by side; the HOTAS One remains the safe recommendation for anyone who wants a single buy that covers MSFS 2024, X-Plane and DCS World casual play without a second thought.

## Setup tips for MSFS 2024

A few practical notes from spending time with this stick across MSFS, X-Plane and DCS:

- **MSFS 2024 auto-detects the HOTAS One** with a pre-built profile. Most pilots tweak only the throttle inversion and the hat switch view assignment.
- **On Xbox**, plug it into the USB port; no driver install required. PC needs the latest Thrustmaster firmware utility — it pushes 10-bit support and the latest detents.
- **Rebind the trigger** to brakes or the parking brake before flying GA — the default mapping varies by sim and is the single most common new-user gotcha.
- **If twist rudder is contaminating your roll**, swap to the **progressive tilting rudder lever** at the base using the supplied hex key. It locks one mode and frees the other.
