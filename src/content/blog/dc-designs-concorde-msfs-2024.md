DC Designs is bringing its acclaimed Concorde addon back to life with a full native rebuild for Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024. Originally released for MSFS 2020 in March 2022, the new version has been rebuilt from the ground up to take advantage of the sim's latest engine capabilities. With a highly realistic Mach 2 flight model, a meticulously detailed 3D cockpit, and one of the most complex fuel systems ever modeled in a flight sim addon, this is shaping up to be a landmark release for virtual aviation enthusiasts.

![DC Designs Concorde flying supersonically in MSFS 2024](../../assets/hero-dc-designs-concorde-msfs-2024.webp)

## From MSFS 2020 to a Native MSFS 2024 Rebuild

The original DC Designs Concorde for MSFS 2020 was already one of the most ambitious addons on the marketplace. It earned praise for capturing the spirit of the supersonic airliner in a way that no other modern sim had managed. However, when MSFS 2024 launched with its overhauled engine, updated rendering pipeline, and new aircraft framework, DC Designs made the decision to rebuild the Concorde natively rather than simply porting the existing model.

That decision means reworking every system, every texture, and every animation to take full advantage of the new platform. The aircraft is already flying in MSFS 2024, with core systems adapted and final development underway. According to DC Designs, the goal is not just parity with the 2020 version but a significant step forward in fidelity and immersion.

## A Cockpit Built Switch by Switch

The Concorde's cockpit was one of the most complex ever designed for a commercial aircraft, and DC Designs has modeled it with extraordinary attention to detail. Every switch, dial, and gauge in the flight deck is rendered in full 3D, creating a cockpit environment that rewards close inspection. The overhead panel alone is dense with systems controls, and the iconic engineer's panel along the right side of the cockpit faithfully reproduces the array of instruments that managed Concorde's unique flight envelope.

![Concorde cockpit in MSFS 2024 with full 3D instrumentation](https://dcdesigns.uk/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/Concorde3-scaled.jpg)

The cockpit includes a working weather radar and a Flight Management Computer (FMC) for route programming. Full VR compatibility ensures that headset users can reach every control naturally, making the experience of flying Concorde from the left seat as immersive as possible. Rain effects on the windshield add another layer of atmospheric realism during approaches in poor weather.

## The Fuel System: 138 Lines, 64 Pumps, 38 Valves

Perhaps the most impressive technical achievement in the DC Designs Concorde is its fuel transfer system. On the real aircraft, fuel was continuously moved between tanks during flight to manage the center of gravity as the aircraft accelerated through the transonic regime and into supersonic cruise. DC Designs has modeled this system in full: 138 fuel lines, 64 pumps, and 38 valves work together to simulate the complex ballet of fuel management that Concorde demanded.

For pilots who want to engage with this system manually, every pump and valve is operable from the engineer's panel. For those who prefer to focus on flying, an automated fuel transfer option handles the process in the background, shifting fuel fore and aft as speed and altitude change. This dual approach makes the aircraft accessible to casual simmers while still offering deep systems depth for study-level enthusiasts.

## Mach 2 at 60,000 Feet

The flight model captures what made Concorde unique among airliners: the ability to cruise at Mach 2 above 60,000 feet, where the curvature of the Earth becomes visible and the sky darkens toward the edge of space. DC Designs has worked to reproduce the aircraft's distinctive flight characteristics, from the high angle of attack on approach (aided by the famous droop nose) to the smooth acceleration through the sound barrier with reheat engaged.

![Concorde in British Airways livery flying over clouds in MSFS](https://dcdesigns.uk/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/Concorde1-scaled.jpg)

Custom animated visual effects enhance the experience. Vapor cones form around the fuselage during transonic flight, reheat plumes glow from the four Olympus 593 engines, and smoke trails stream behind during takeoff. The native sound package has been designed specifically for MSFS 2024, reproducing the distinctive roar and rumble that made Concorde unmistakable from the ground. Reflective glass on the cockpit windows adds subtle visual polish that benefits from the sim's improved lighting system.

## Liveries and Cabin Detail

The addon ships with four historically accurate liveries covering both operators of the aircraft:

- **British Airways 1985-1987** -- the classic BA scheme with the dark blue tail
- **British Airways 1997-2003** -- the Chatham Dockyard Union Flag design worn in Concorde's final years
- **Air France 1976-2003** -- the tricolor livery that served throughout Air France's Concorde operations
- **Singapore Airlines G-BOAD** -- the unique joint BA/Singapore Airlines livery carried by G-BOAD on services to Singapore

Beyond the exterior, DC Designs has modeled the forward passenger cabin with period-accurate seating and working doors. Crew figures are present in the cabin, adding life to the aircraft when viewed from the passenger perspective. An interactive ground crew system rounds out the experience on the ramp, with jetway connections, a fuel truck, power truck, baggage handling, and catering vehicles all animated and functional.

## What This Means for Simmers

At $39.99 on [Just Flight](https://dcdesigns.uk/concorde/), the DC Designs Concorde occupies a compelling price point for an aircraft of this complexity. The combination of deep systems modeling, visual fidelity, and historical accuracy makes it one of the most anticipated addons for MSFS 2024.

DC Designs has also confirmed that Career Mode support is planned for a post-launch update, which would allow pilots to build a flying career around the Concorde within the sim's progression system. This integration with MSFS 2024's native career framework could add significant replay value and a structured way to experience the aircraft over time.

Looking ahead, DC Designs has announced that the F-16 will be its next MSFS 2024 project, followed by the F-14 Tomcat. Both military jets suggest that the developer's ambitions extend well beyond civilian aviation, but for now, the Concorde remains the flagship product and a showcase for what a native MSFS 2024 addon can achieve.

## A Worthy Return for a Supersonic Icon

Concorde occupies a singular place in aviation history -- the only supersonic airliner to achieve sustained commercial service, and a symbol of an era when speed mattered more than fuel economy. DC Designs' native rebuild for MSFS 2024 aims to honor that legacy with the most detailed digital Concorde ever created for a consumer flight simulator.

With the aircraft already flying in the sim and final development in progress, the release should not be far off. For virtual pilots who have dreamed of pushing past Mach 2 at the edge of the stratosphere, this is the addon to watch.
