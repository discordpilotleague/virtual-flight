The Rafale M is one of those freeware projects that forces you to recalibrate your expectations. Developed by **alpha60** and available for free on [flightsim.to](https://flightsim.to/file/17785/dassault-rafale-m), this add-on for **MSFS 2024** and **MSFS 2020** recreates the French Navy's carrier-based fighter with a level of polish that would put many payware products to shame. With over **123,000 downloads** and a **4.5/5 rating** from 209 reviews, alpha60's Rafale M has established itself as one of the most popular military add-ons in the Microsoft Flight Simulator ecosystem. Version 3.6, released on February 18, 2026, continues five years of ongoing development.

![Dassault Rafale M in flight with Tiger Meet livery — MSFS 2024](/images/dassault-rafale-m-msfs/hero.webp)

## A Converted Model, Then Deeply Reworked

alpha60's Rafale M originated as a conversion of Thor's Hammer's FSX model, carried out with the original author's permission. But reducing this project to a mere conversion would be a mistake. Over successive updates, alpha60 completely reworked the model in **Blender**, improving the graphics, animations, and textures to reach a visual quality worthy of MSFS 2024. The 3D cockpit by **Johnnypropeller**, textures and liveries by **MartyF909**, and the landing gear and external fuel tank modeling by alpha60 himself form a cohesive package — the result of a genuine collaborative effort.

![Rafale M side view on the ground on an aircraft carrier — MSFS](/images/dassault-rafale-m-msfs/exterior.webp)

## 3D Cockpit and Onboard Systems

The Rafale M's cockpit is not just a visual shell. The 3D instrument panel integrates the front-sector optronics (OSF), flight instruments, and a realistic startup sequence that requires following a precise order: battery, avionics, engine valves, APU-GAP, then left and right engine ignition. Shutdown is more straightforward — simply cut the engine valves. This level of procedure, unusual for a military freeware under MSFS, adds a welcome layer of immersion.

The autopilot is fully functional, and the fuel management system includes automated jettisoning of the 2,000-liter external fuel tanks. Night vision, added in 2024, rounds out the onboard systems arsenal.

## Flight Performance: From 100 Knots to Mach 1.7

The Rafale M's flight envelope covers an impressive range. The aircraft remains maneuverable from 100 knots in low-speed configuration and can reach **Mach 1.7 at FL500** in clean configuration. This broad flight envelope allows pilots to replicate everything from slow carrier approaches to high-altitude intercepts.

The loadout configuration is customizable. Virtual pilots can adjust the weapons and external fuel load before each flight, directly affecting the aircraft's performance and handling — a detail that military simulation enthusiasts will appreciate.

![Rafale M in flight through clouds at sunset — Marine nationale livery](/images/dassault-rafale-m-msfs/cockpit.webp)

## Carrier Operations

The "M" suffix on the Rafale designates the Marine variant, designed for catapult launches and arrested landings on French Navy aircraft carriers — primarily the Charles de Gaulle. alpha60's add-on replicates these operations: catapult-assisted takeoff and arrestor wire hookup on landing are both simulated. For pilots looking to practice naval procedures in MSFS, this is one of the few freeware add-ons that delivers this capability.

![Rafale M on the flight deck of an aircraft carrier — MSFS 2024](/images/dassault-rafale-m-msfs/livery.webp)

## Liveries: From Tactical Grey to Tiger Meet

One of this add-on's strongest features is the richness of its livery collection. Beyond the standard French Navy grey scheme, the Rafale M offers several special decorations:

- **Tiger Meet 2022** — striking blue livery with a stylized tiger on the tail fin
- **Tiger Meet 2024** — bold red variant with a top-down tiger head design
- **Portugal Solo Display** — tricolor blue-white-red decoration
- **Brazil** — livery in Brazilian colors
- **Camouflage** — tactical camouflage scheme
- **Marine Grey** — the regulation fleet grey

These liveries, crafted by MartyF909, are a genuine visual treat and keep things fresh across your flights.

## Compatibility and Installation

The Rafale M is compatible with **MSFS 2020** and **MSFS 2024**. The file weighs in at **1.13 GB** — a reasonable size given the texture quality and number of included liveries. Installation follows the standard flightsim.to add-on process: extract into the simulator's Community folder.
