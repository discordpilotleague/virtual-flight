Microsoft and Asobo Studio just dropped one of the most anticipated regional refreshes of the year. World Update 21 turns Australia into a fully reworked playground for Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024 and 2020 — 40 photogrammetry-enhanced cities, six handcrafted airports, dozens of new points of interest, and a fresh batch of missions. All of it free.

![Sydney rebuilt with photogrammetry in MSFS 2024 World Update 21](../../assets/hero-msfs-2024-world-update-21-australia.webp)

## A long-awaited makeover for the southern continent

Australia had not received a major content pass since World Update 7 in January 2021 — an eternity in flight sim time. The MSFS team partnered with Bing Maps, Vexcel and Gaya to refresh the entire country with current photogrammetry data and new TIN (triangulated irregular network) surface texturing. The visual jump is striking: Sydney Opera House, the Twelve Apostles, the rust-red expanses of the outback and the rainforests of Queensland now read at the same level of fidelity as the European and North American flagships from earlier updates.

Available since May 5, 2026, the update installs through the Content Manager on both MSFS 2020 and MSFS 2024 — no Premium edition required. It applies to every platform: PC, Xbox Series X|S, and Xbox Cloud Gaming.

## 40 areas of interest across all states and territories

The 40 AOIs span every Australian state and the two federal territories. New South Wales gets the lion's share with eleven cities including Sydney, Newcastle-Maitland, the Blue Mountains, and Wagga Wagga. Queensland follows with ten — Brisbane, the Gold Coast, Cairns, Townsville and Mackay among them. Victoria adds eight (Melbourne, Geelong, Bendigo), Western Australia five (Perth, Mandurah), South Australia two (Adelaide), Tasmania two (Hobart, Launceston), plus Canberra in the ACT and Darwin in the Northern Territory.

Smaller satellite suburbs like Springwood, Chatswood, Minto, Joondalup, Mooroolbark and Woodcroft also made the cut. The takeaway: Australia is no longer a "couple of capitals plus blurry interior" deal — it now reads as a coherent country at low altitude. Dozens of new POIs (parks, dams, lighthouses, observatories, major buildings) have been hand-placed on top of the photogrammetry layer to give VFR pilots clear visual landmarks.

## Six handcrafted airports — and Lord Howe Island

The headline airports are all built by Gaya, the Israeli studio behind several recent World Update reworks. They are spread to encourage cross-country flying:

- **Broken Hill (YBHI)** — outback New South Wales, Royal Flying Doctor Service base, gateway to the Silver City.
- **Broome (YBRM)** — international gateway to the Kimberley region in north-western Australia.
- **Coober Pedy (YCBP)** — South Australia's iconic opal-mining town, with red-earth surroundings.
- **Bathurst (YBTH)** — central tablelands of NSW, classic regional GA airfield.
- **RAAF Base Tindal / Katherine (YPTN)** — dual-use military and civilian, Northern Territory.
- **Lord Howe Island (YLHI)** — the legendary light-aircraft transit between Australia and New Zealand, with a runway that demands respect.

Each airport ships with custom buildings, accurate ground markings, animated taxiway lights, and PBR materials. Lord Howe alone is worth the download — its short runway nestled between volcanic peaks turns every approach into a mini-event.

## Missions, discovery flights and a brand-new bush trip

MSFS 2024 owners get the full mission package: three Discovery Flights (Canberra, Cocos Islands, Whitsunday Islands), five Precision Landings — including helipad approaches at Mount Wedge and South Solitary Island — two Low Altitude Challenges over Lake Eildon and Kimbolton, and two Rally Race Challenges around the Three Sisters and the Twelve Apostles. The aircraft roster mixes a Boeing 737, an Airbus A320, the F/A-18 Super Hornet, the CubCrafters XCub, the Robinson R66 and the Aero Vodochody L-39 Albatros — something for every cockpit preference.

MSFS 2020 keeps the three Discovery Flights, three Landing Challenges (Broome, Coober Pedy, Tindal), and adds a full **bush trip along the Great Ocean Road** — the type of slow-paced, leg-by-leg adventure that built the original sim's identity. Expect spectacular VFR scenery between Apollo Bay, the Twelve Apostles and Port Campbell.

## Why this matters for virtual pilots

Beyond the obvious "free DLC, take it" angle, this update fixes a real gap in the sim. Australian-based virtual airlines, VATPAC and IVAO Oceania members have spent five years routing around an outdated photogrammetry layer; that excuse is gone. The mix of dense urban areas (Sydney, Melbourne) and immense empty stretches (between Coober Pedy and Tindal you can fly an hour without a single 500-foot landmark) makes Australia one of the most varied training grounds in the entire sim.

Helicopter pilots in particular get a renewed playground: Mount Wedge and South Solitary Island helipads, the Sydney Harbour skyline, the Whitsundays — all the photo flights you used to do in real life are now back on the menu. And the Lord Howe approach is mandatory homework for anyone preparing for short-runway certifications.

## Getting started

Launch MSFS 2024 (or 2020), open the **Marketplace → World Updates** tab and grab "World Update 21: Australia". Allow 30–60 GB of disk headroom depending on your existing photogrammetry cache. Once installed, restart the sim, then either pick a Discovery Flight from the activities menu or load directly into one of the six new airports. Real-time Bing data and live weather will show you Australia in current daylight conditions — which, given the eight-hour offset from Europe, often means a glorious Pacific dawn.

The official rollout details, screenshots and trailer are on the [Microsoft Flight Simulator World Update 21 page](https://www.flightsimulator.com/world-update-21/). For mission and bush trip walkthroughs, [FSElite](https://fselite.net/) and the [official Asobo dev blog](https://www.flightsimulator.com/category/news/) will publish guides over the coming days.

## Conclusion

World Update 21 is a textbook example of what a modern free world refresh should be: meaningful visual upgrade, six airports of real flying value, missions for every player profile, and a bush trip that nods to the heritage of the simulator. After five years of waiting, Australian virtual pilots are finally back on the world map — and everyone else has a brand-new continent to discover. Set the autopilot to YSSY and start exploring.
