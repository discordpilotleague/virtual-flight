Every flight simulator pilot keeps score in some way — landing rate, fuel burn, hours flown. **[PilotLeague](https://pilotleague.com/en/)** has just rolled out the final design of its **Achievement Cards** system, and the new look turns those numbers into a proper collection. 47 cards, 4 rarities, 50 levels and 16 competitive ranks now sit behind a TCG-style grid that rewards everything from a first take-off to a Level 50 milestone.

![PilotLeague Achievements page with Sky Regular, Century Pilot, Sky Veteran and Flight Master cards](../../assets/hero-pilotleague-achievements-cards-2026.webp)
*Credit: [PilotLeague Achievements](https://pilots.pilotleague.com/v4/badges)*

## A complete progression system, not just a logbook

PilotLeague now stacks three layers of progression on top of every connected flight:

- **50 levels** — XP earned from flights, landings, fuel discipline and SOP compliance.
- **47 achievements** — one-time and milestone goals, each with a unique illustration and stat.
- **16 competitive ranks** — applied to the rolling 14-day leaderboard, so the ladder stays alive.

The point of the new card design is to make that progression **visible**. Instead of a numeric list, every achievement now ships as a printed-card illustration with a rarity frame, a centered stat (50 flights, 10K XP, Centerline < 5 ft) and a progress bar underneath. The cards are designed to feel like something you could put in a binder — and they read at a glance whether you are mid-flight reviewing your goals or scrolling the badges page after landing.

If you are new to the platform, our earlier [PilotLeague flight analysis guide](/blog/pilotleague-flight-analysis-msfs-2024) explains the underlying scoring engine that feeds every XP gain shown on these cards.

## Four rarities, four visual languages

The team built four distinct visual treatments — one per rarity — so you know at a glance how rare a card is when you unlock it.

![Legendary, Epic and Rare PilotLeague achievement cards including Perfect Flight and Master Pilot](/images/pilotleague-badges/legendary-epic-rare-cards.webp)
*Credit: [PilotLeague](https://pilots.pilotleague.com/v4/badges)*

- **Common** — gunmetal frame with cool-blue accents. Earned during the first dozens of flights: First Flight (+50 XP), First Landing, First Hour, Profile Complete, Runway Rookie, Butter Landing under 50 fpm (+100 XP).
- **Rare** — silver-blue frame with crisp typographic stats. Examples: **Sky Regular** at 50 flights (+400 XP), Established Pilot at level 25, Dedicated Aviator at 50 hours, Perfect Landing.
- **Epic** — purple-gold detailing on a dark plate. Includes **Surgeon** (centerline accuracy under 5 ft, +1200 XP), Master Pilot, Long Hauler, Iron Man, Sky Veteran at 250 flights.
- **Legendary** — full-gold frame with laurel motifs. Reserved for the milestones the community will only see a handful of pilots reach: **Legend** at level 50 (+5000 XP), **Perfect Flight** at score 100/100, **Flight Master** at 500 flights.

Each card also shows the XP it grants on unlock, so you can target the next rarity bracket on purpose. The XP curve is steep enough that a single Legendary unlock can move you several levels up the ladder.

## A category-grouped page that scales with the season

The Achievements page itself was redesigned around the cards. A sticky header summarizes how many cards you have unlocked in each rarity (the chips show **Legendary / Epic / Rare / Common** counters), three filter tabs let you switch between **All**, **In Progress** and **Locked**, and the grid below is grouped by category — Flights, Landings, Hours, SOP, Safety, Exploration, Milestones.

![Common PilotLeague achievement cards: First Flight, First Landing, First Hour, Profile Complete](/images/pilotleague-badges/common-cards-foundation.webp)
*Credit: [PilotLeague](https://pilots.pilotleague.com/v4/badges)*

Three details make the new page hold up over a long season:

1. **Progress bars under locked cards** show exactly where you are — 38/50 flights, 8 700 / 10 000 XP, 4.2 ft / 5 ft centerline — so unlocking never feels random.
2. **Unlocked cards drop the progress bar** and become collectible-clean, with the rarity frame doing all the talking.
3. **Light and dark mode** were re-tuned together; the rarity colors stay readable on both themes, and the grid keeps the same density on mobile and desktop.

The desktop overlay app and the public profile page both pick up the new cards too, so an unlocked Legendary card surfaces in your shareable [pilots.pilotleague.com](https://pilots.pilotleague.com/v4/badges) URL the moment you earn it.

## Examples worth chasing

Five cards capture the spread of the system well:

| Achievement | Rarity | Requirement | XP |
|---|---|---|---|
| First Flight | Common | Complete one tracked flight | +50 |
| Butter Landing | Common | Land below 50 fpm | +100 |
| Sky Regular | Rare | 50 flights | +400 |
| Surgeon | Epic | Centerline under 5 ft on landing | +1200 |
| Legend | Legendary | Reach level 50 | +5000 |

That spread is intentional: the Common tier exists so new pilots see their first card within minutes, the Rare and Epic tiers reward steady week-on-week flying, and the Legendary tier is the season-long carrot. Combine that with the rolling 14-day leaderboard documented in our [PilotLeague Spring 2026 update](/blog/pilotleague-spring-2026-update) and the platform now has the same long-tail feedback loop that drives Strava, Duolingo and Steam achievement hunts. For pilots running their own logbook on the side, our [virtual pilot career logbook guide](/blog/virtual-pilot-career-logbook) compares this kind of in-platform progression with third-party tools like Volanta and SimToolkitPro.

## More cards are coming through the season

PilotLeague has confirmed that **new achievement cards will be added over the course of the season**. Expect themed drops — event-based cards tied to community fly-ins, seasonal Legendary cards that disappear after a window, and category-specific cards for fuel efficiency, SOP, and safety. Because the cards live in the same rarity system, each drop adds collection depth without resetting what you have already unlocked.

The roadmap also includes a card-detail view (artwork close-up, unlock date, rarity stats across the community), and a "next card" widget on the dashboard that surfaces the closest achievement to your current activity. If you fly four times a week, the next Rare card will rarely be far away.

## How to start collecting

To start unlocking cards today, you only need three things:

1. **Install the PilotLeague desktop app** — it auto-tracks flights in MSFS 2024 and supports X-Plane 12 in parallel. The install link is on [pilotleague.com](https://pilotleague.com/en/features/).
2. **Complete your profile** — uploading a profile photo and a callsign instantly unlocks the **Profile Complete** Common card.
3. **Fly something short** — even a 15-minute hop will grant **First Flight** (+50) and, if you land below 50 fpm, **Butter Landing** (+100).

From there, the [Achievements page](https://pilots.pilotleague.com/v4/badges) becomes your map. Sort by **In Progress** and the closest card to unlock will be at the top of the grid.

## Conclusion

The new Achievement Cards are the biggest visual update PilotLeague has shipped since the public profiles rolled out. They turn every flight into a tangible step toward a collection, they hold up across rarities, and they leave plenty of room for the platform to grow throughout the season. For virtual pilots who like the feeling of building something across MSFS and X-Plane sessions, this is exactly the kind of feedback loop the genre has been missing — and at zero cost, it is an easy add to your weekly flying. Watch the [PilotLeague features page](https://pilotleague.com/en/features/) for the next card drop.
