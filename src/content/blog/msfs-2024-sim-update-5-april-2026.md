Microsoft has released **Sim Update 5 (SU5)** for Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024 on April 30, 2026. The update is free for every owner of the simulator and lands at the same time as the long-awaited **PSVR2 launch on PlayStation 5**, the unlocking of the **Heavy and VTOL Career Pilot specializations**, a brand-new ATC queueing system, a wide set of performance and stability fixes, and several quality-of-life touches across VR, avionics and the world map. This article walks through everything you need to know — the changelog, what it means for your virtual flying habits, and how to get the most out of SU5 in the coming weeks.

## What is Sim Update 5?

Sim Update 5 is the fifth major free patch since the November 2024 launch of MSFS 2024 and continues the cadence Asobo Studio set in 2025: roughly one large platform update per quarter, paired with smaller City Updates and Local Legends in between. SU5 is **version SU_05**, and it ships at the same time as **City Update 14 (Netherlands & Belgium)** and **Local Legend 23 (Fokker F27)**, both sold separately on the Marketplace. The headline of this release is on the platform side rather than on new content: better frame rates in dense scenery, smoother VR, a more credible ATC, and the unlocking of long-promised Career Pilot specializations.

The update is mandatory: as soon as you launch the simulator after April 30, the platform will download and install SU5 before letting you fly. Allow about 10 to 25 GB of bandwidth depending on your installed editions and add-ons, and at least 30 minutes of patch time on a typical connection.

## PSVR2 launch and major VR improvements

The biggest single news of SU5 is the arrival of **PSVR2 on PlayStation 5**. MSFS 2024 is one of the very few flight simulators available natively on console VR, and Sony's headset gives PS5 owners a 4K-per-eye OLED experience with eye tracking and headset HDR — a level of fidelity that was previously only available on high-end PC headsets like the Pimax Crystal or Varjo Aero. The PSVR2 build is included free of charge for current owners of MSFS 2024 on PS5.

For VR pilots in general, SU5 introduces a clean **HUD toggle** that disables the head-up display in VR while keeping it on the 2D screen for spectators or streamers. **Tooltip cards** for cockpit switches have also been repositioned closer to the actual control, which significantly reduces the eye-strain of looking at a label floating in the middle of nowhere. These are small touches, but they make long IFR sessions in VR a lot more comfortable.

## Career Pilot: Heavy and VTOL unlocked, smarter ATC

The other big-ticket feature is the unlocking of **Heavy and VTOL aircraft specializations** in Career Pilot mode. Players who grinded through cargo, charter and bush operations during the first 18 months of MSFS 2024 can finally upgrade their virtual licence to operate large jets and helicopters in the same career flow. Mission spawning has been reworked so the dispatcher will no longer try to park a 777 in a Cessna-sized parking spot.

ATC also gets a big upgrade. The new **ATC queueing system prioritizes player requests** over background AI traffic, which fixes one of the most-complained-about behaviors of MSFS 2024 since launch — having to wait three or four AI aircraft before being cleared for departure. Runway light detection at night and runway length validation are now smarter, and ground services are independent: a stuck fuel truck no longer blocks pushback, catering or the GPU.

## Avionics, weather radar and the modding side

Avionics enthusiasts get a treat with the addition of the **Avidyne IFD540 and IFD550 cockpit** on the Cessna 172 Skyhawk (Classic), available as a high-fidelity alternative to the default Garmin GTN setup. The IFD series is widely used in real-world general aviation, and having it as a first-party option opens up a more authentic GA experience without buying a payware aircraft.

Behind the scenes, SU5 also exposes a brand-new **Weather Radar API** to third-party developers, including support for radar tilt — a feature long requested by airliner add-on makers like PMDG, iniBuilds and Fenix. Expect a wave of weather-radar updates from these studios in the months that follow, since the platform now does the heavy lifting and developers can focus on the cockpit-side display logic.

## Performance and AMD RX 9xxx fix

If you fly out of large cities or busy hubs, SU5 should feel noticeably smoother. Asobo's notes specifically call out **frame-rate gains and stutter reductions** in dense urban scenery and complex airports. On the GPU side, the team has fixed a long-standing exposure glitch on **AMD Radeon RX 9000-series cards**, where the screen would flicker brighter or darker on certain transitions. Owners of the Radeon RX 9070, 9080 and 9090 should see consistent exposure from now on.

Other quality-of-life improvements include the **aircraft variation types now visible in the selector** (so you can tell at a glance whether you're picking the standard, glass-cockpit or upgraded version), a **sim rate display under the mini-map**, a new **Global Rendering Quality settings page** that gathers all rendering options in one place, and an **Air Flow Visualization** section for users who want to inspect the aerodynamic model of their aircraft.

## What it means for virtual pilots

For day-to-day MSFS 2024 users, SU5 should feel like a polish pass that finally gets the sim where it should have been at launch. The **ATC priority queue** alone changes the rhythm of every short hop you do, since you no longer wait for a procedural conga line of AI aircraft before getting your taxi clearance. The **Career Pilot Heavy and VTOL** unlock is significant for anyone who built up a virtual airline-style pilot in 2025 and was waiting for the next career chapter — heavy and helicopter operations are now full-fledged tracks with their own missions and progression.

VR users get a clearly more polished experience: the **HUD toggle** is one of those features you don't realize you needed until you have it, and the **tooltip repositioning** removes one of the few real ergonomic frustrations that remained in cockpit VR. PS5 owners who haven't tried VR yet have the cheapest first-party PSVR2 entry into serious flight simulation that has ever existed.

Finally, the **Weather Radar API** is a quiet but very important update for the add-on ecosystem. Within a few months, expect PMDG's 737 family, iniBuilds' A350, the Fenix A320 and others to receive proper radar tilt logic that interacts correctly with MSFS weather. This is the kind of platform plumbing that ages a sim well.

## How to make the most of SU5

If you don't have the time to read the full changelog, here is the practical checklist:

- **Update first**, then go to **Options → Graphics** and check the new **Global Rendering Quality** page. Asobo introduced presets that may be worth re-baselining on your hardware.
- If you fly in VR, toggle the new **HUD off** in the VR settings — most veteran VR pilots prefer the cleaner cockpit view.
- In **Career Pilot**, talk to your dispatcher to unlock the **Heavy** and **VTOL** specializations. Both require a check ride that itself is worth flying for the experience.
- If you own an **AMD RX 9000 GPU**, install the latest Adrenalin driver in addition to SU5 — the fix relies on the up-to-date driver to expose consistent gamma.
- **General aviation lovers**: try the **Avidyne IFD540 cockpit** on the Cessna 172 Skyhawk Classic. It's a real-world avionics suite that handles flight planning very differently from the Garmin family.
- **Heavy aircraft pilots**: wait a few weeks before flying long-haul if your favorite payware add-on hasn't released its SU5 patch yet — the new Weather Radar API and the avionics tweaks may need a small developer-side update.

## Final thoughts

Sim Update 5 is one of the most substantive free patches MSFS 2024 has received so far. It is platform-focused rather than content-focused, but the topics it tackles — performance, ATC, Career Pilot scope, VR ergonomics — are exactly the ones that the community has been calling for since launch. Combined with PSVR2 going live on PS5 the same day, SU5 also widens MSFS 2024's reach beyond the PC gaming demographic, which is good news for the long-term health of the platform.

The full release notes and all the side announcements are on the [official Microsoft Flight Simulator blog](https://www.flightsimulator.com/microsoft-flight-simulator-2024-releases-sim-update-5/), and the standalone **City Update 14 (Netherlands & Belgium)** and **Local Legend 23 (Fokker F27)** are <!-- nofollow --> [available on the Marketplace](https://www.flightsimulator.com/marketplace/) for owners who want to pair SU5 with new flying grounds.
